Why this exists

Universal dictation for builders who do not always think in one language.

DictaHue starts where adoption starts: fast, private English dictation that works in the apps you already use. Then it handles the real builder pattern: product terms, acronyms, mixed accents, and language switching.

Hindi+English is the first multilingual mode because that is where local dictation still feels weak. The product should expand language by language without making English slower or less reliable.

1

Hold the hotkey

A tiny orb shows that the app is listening. It stays out of your way.

2

Speak normally

English, Indian English, acronyms, app names, rambling, and Hindi+English when you switch.

3

Release to paste

Useful text lands in the focused field. If no field is focused, a compact copy toast appears.

Backed by real recordings

Keeps the meaning, keeps the terms, stays fast.

We replay real saved recordings through the local engine and compare the output with Gemini-cross-checked gold transcripts. The takeaway: English is daily-use ready, and Hindi+English preserves more meaning than the tested local alternatives.
